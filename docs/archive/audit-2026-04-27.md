# Monogatari Corpus — Literary Audit
**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Scope:** All 56 shipped stories
**Method:** Three independent literary editors (subagents), same rubric, parallel reads of stories 1–19 / 20–38 / 39–56. Synthesis by reviewing agent.

This audit was triggered by a quality complaint on `story_6` whose JP text is grammatical and validates clean but reads as pseudo-poetic nonsense ("the cat's color is the color of the rain"). The hypothesis going in was: this is one bad story. The audit shows it is a recurring **category** of defect that the deterministic validators were not built to catch.

## 2. The five recurring defects

Patterns that fired in 3+ stories across all three audits. Each is a candidate for a deterministic lint rule (#1–#4) or a literary-review gate (#5).

### 2.1 noun-pile-closer — fired in ≥15 stories
**Pattern:** closer sentence is a list of nouns + です with no verb. Form: `Aや B、Adj N です` or `A、B、C です`.
**Examples:**
- #3 s5 — 「雨の朝、温かい朝ごはん、いい気分です。」
- #4 s6 — 「夕方の散歩、石や花、いい気分です。」
- #6 s7 — 「雨の朝、猫や花、静かな窓です。」
- #20 s8 — 「外は雪、痛い手紙、温かい朝です。」
- #27 s8 — 「暑い夏、美しい庭、嬉しい一人の朝です。」
- #53 s14 — 「新しい年の朝、古い本、温かいお茶です。」
- #55 s19 — 「玄関に花、庭の中の気持ちです。」
**Why it slipped:** `validate.py` counts tokens, not sentence shape. 100% mechanically valid.

### 2.2 bare-known-fact (`〜と思います` on a perceived fact) — fired in ≥10 stories
**Pattern:** narrator hedges with 思います about something already asserted as physically present in the same story.
**Examples:**
- #12 s4 — 「私は朝だと思います。」 (said in the morning)
- #17 s9 — 「『友達が来ますから、嬉しい』と私は思います。」 (joy hedged about an event that just happened)
- #19 s5 — 「『鳥は元気ですか』と私はベンチで思います。」 (about a bird actively doing things in front of narrator)
- #27 s7 — 「夏の庭は美しい」 (said standing in summer garden)
- #30 s11 — 「友達の母も元気ですか」 (no information added)
- #32 s8 — 「夏は暑い、冬は寒いですね」 (universal fact recast as discovery)
**Why it slipped:** `semantic_lint.py` rule 11.3 has a NARROW guard requiring "exactly one content noun in the と-clause." Real-world cases use full embedded sentences, which slip past.

### 2.3 misapplied-quiet (静か on inanimate non-place) — fired in ≥9 stories
**Pattern:** scene-noun (rain / moon / stone / letter) + は + 静か + です/だ. Adverbial 静かに on the same nouns counts too.
**Examples:**
- #1 s4 — 「雨は静かです。」
- #4 s2 — 「石は静かです。」
- #6 s3 — 「猫は静かに雨の色を見ます。」 (adverbial form bypasses 11.1)
- #7 s2 — 「月は静かです。」
- #13 s5 — 「『青い雨は静かだ』と思います。」 (inside quoted clause)
- #21 s5 — 「道の角は静かです。」
- #54 s12 — 「心の中の気持ちは、静かな時間です。」 (abstract noun = quiet time)
**Why it slipped:** rule 11.1 requires literal `静か です/だ` adjacency. Adverbial and quoted-clause forms slip past.

### 2.4 tautological-equivalence (`XのY は ZのY です`) — fired in ≥8 stories
**Pattern:** same noun on both sides of は + copula, only the possessor changes. Reads "literary," asserts nothing.
**Examples:**
- #6 s6 — 「猫の色は、雨の色です。」
- #11 s5 — 「友達の手紙の色は、月の色です。」
- #40 s11 — 「好きな人の名前は思い出です。」
- #47 s10 — 「本の店の人です。」 (referent collapses on itself)
- #53 s8 — 「この時計の時間は古いです。」
- #54 s12 — 「心の中の気持ちは、静かな時間です。」 (also misapplied-quiet)
**Why it slipped:** there is no rule for it at all. Easiest to add, highest yield per line of code.

### 2.5 missing-arc (no beginning/middle/end, just images) — fired in ≥13 stories
**Pattern:** stories that read as a list of disconnected sentences sharing only setting; no causality, no change of state, no payoff.
**Examples:** 22, 24, 28, 31, 35, 38, 39, 40, 43, 50, 54, 55.
**Why it slipped:** not a sentence-level defect. Cannot be caught by token-pattern matching. **This needs a literary-review gate, not a lint rule.**

## 3. The motif cycle

All three audits independently flagged it. Two distinct cycles:

**Cycle A — early corpus (~stories 6–14, drifting into 24/25/28/36):**
rain + cat + window + letter + moon. ~14 stories share this prop bag.

**Cycle B — late corpus (~stories 39–55):**
bookstore + tea + grandmother + warm emotion + bell. Audit 3: *"the motif no longer feels like a world; it feels like a template."*

`motif_rotation_lint`'s 3-story look-back window cannot catch either: the cycles drift in and out across many more stories than the window covers.

## 4. Budget regrets — words that arrived too late

Words whose late introduction *forced* authors into metaphor and hedging. Sorted by estimated rescue value.

| Lemma | Currently introduced | Should ship by | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **小さい** (small) | story 7 | story 3–5 | Concrete size adjective; eliminates "small wind" / "small star" hedging |
| **赤い / 黄色い / 白い** (concrete colors) | white at 13, others never | story 5–8 | Lets 色 be used concretely instead of "X's color is Y's color" tautology |
| **下 / そば / 中** (spatial: below/beside/inside) | mostly never or very late | story 5–7 | Story 6 had to invent "the cat is rain-colored" because there's no way to say "the cat is *under* the awning" |
| **明るい** (bright) | story 16 | story 8–10 | Replaces hedged sky/light descriptions |
| **causality connectors** (ので, から-reason, だから) | thinly | by story 15 | Stories 28+ stall at narrative logic |
| **dialogue verbs** (言います, 話します properly) | thinly | by story 12 | 思います is forced to do all psychological work; this *creates* the bare-known-fact defect |
| **concrete action verbs** (置く, 開ける, 持つ, 立つ, 座る, 寝る) | scattered, mostly late | by story 10 | Lets stories *act* instead of *observe* |

## 5. What the SHIP list teaches

The 18 ship-ready stories cluster around three structural choices:
1. **A concrete object as anchor** — #26 (key), #34 (dog), #29 (bench), #45 (box), #56 (leaf/bridge). The object forces causality.
2. **Two characters with a shared task** — #10 (letter exchange), #16 (bread → bird arrives), #23 (station meeting), #42 (walk together), #52 (teacher visits).
3. **A single sensory beat sustained** — #1 (rain), #5 (morning walk), #41 (spring bookstore), #46 (not-cold morning).

The defective stories almost universally fail one of these: no anchor object, no second character with stake, or a single beat dragged out into 7+ disconnected images.

## 6. Honest limitations of this audit

- The three editors share a rubric. They probably **over-flag** poetic-compression closers in stories where it works (e.g., #25 s8 was flagged but the editor itself noted it was "thematically coherent"). Tighten by hand.
- Performed from English glosses + JP. A native reader might disagree on milder calls (especially #1 s4 「雨は静かです」 which many natives accept as standard pathetic-fallacy idiom).
- "missing-arc" is the most subjective category. Some flagged stories may be defensible as "vignettes."
- The audit doesn't measure pedagogical value — only literary quality. A story can be a slop-fest and still teach a grammar point well.

## 7. Recommendations — Phase 2 inputs

In priority order:

1. **Codify defects 2.1, 2.2, 2.3 (loosened), 2.4 as deterministic lint rules.** ~80 lines of code in `pipeline/semantic_lint.py`.
2. **Reorder vocab introduction in `data/vocab_state.json`** to land §4's top six lemmas earlier. Estimated cascade: ~10 stories need re-validation, ~5 need real edits.
3. **Add a literary-review gate** for missing-arc (defect 2.5) — a structured prompt that runs *before* a story is allowed into `stories/`. The current `engagement_baseline.json` infrastructure is the right place.
4. **Cut decision: 21 REWRITE-STORY entries → keep ~10, retire/renumber the rest.** A corpus where every story earns its slot is better than a corpus padded with broken stories.
5. **Hand-author north-star sentences for stories 1–10** — the new word-ladder is set up to enable concrete prose; the opening stories need to *demonstrate* it so later authors (human or LLM) have something to imitate.

---

## 8. What this audit changes about the project

- The `semantic_lint.py` docstring already says "we add a rule when we find a defect." This audit added 4 more defect categories. Adding 4 more rules is following the project's own design philosophy.
- The `motif_rotation_lint` look-back window of 3 is empirically too short. Either widen the window or move motif-tracking to a corpus-wide check.
- The "engagement reviewer" alluded to in `semantic_lint.py` comments needs to actually exist as code, not just as a manual checklist. Without it, missing-arc cannot be caught.
